Behavioral Insights

🚬 Quit Smoking — Biggest Modifiable Win

Smokers face significantly elevated cardiovascular risk. Quitting smoking can reduce heart disease risk by up to 50% within one year of cessation, making it the single highest-impact lifestyle change.

🏃 30 Minutes Daily Changes Everything

High physical activity patients in this dataset consistently show the lowest disease rates across all age groups. Even moderate activity (150 min/week) produces measurable risk reduction.

🧘 Chronic Stress Damages the Heart

High stress elevates cortisol, which raises blood pressure and promotes inflammation and clotting. Stress management interventions are increasingly recognized as direct cardiovascular interventions.

🥗 Diet Quality Compounds Over Time

Patients with poor diet quality show elevated cholesterol, higher BMI, and greater BP — all upstream of heart disease. The Mediterranean diet pattern is supported by strongest evidence.

🍷 Alcohol: J-Curve Relationship

Moderate alcohol shows some protective associations in literature, but heavy drinking clearly elevates risk. This dataset shows a modest association between drinking and elevated disease rate.

📈 Risk is Multiplicative

Patients with 3 or more negative lifestyle factors (smoking + inactivity + poor diet + stress) show dramatically higher disease rates — demonstrating how risks compound rather than simply add.
